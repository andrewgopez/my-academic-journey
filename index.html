Finding Myself Through Failure

From struggling in Computer Science to finding where I truly belong

I started CEGEP convinced I would build my future in Computer Science. But over time, I realized I was struggling more than I expected—and I began to question whether I was even on the right path. This timeline follows how that struggle turned into growth, leadership, and eventually discovering where I truly belong.

✨ Tip: Scroll through each event to explore

💡 Pre-CEGEP

Where it Began

Growing up, I was always fascinated by technology and how computers worked. My dad works in the tech industry, and I often saw him at work. When I showed interest in coding, he taught me how to create a website using HTML, a language used to structure and build web pages. During the COVID-19 pandemic, with plenty of free time at home, he encouraged me to relearn HTML and teach myself CSS and JavaScript—the languages that add style and interactivity to websites. By 2022, this passion led me to apply to the Computer Science program at John Abbott College. Before I started, he suggested I also learn C#, the main programming language used in the program, to get a head start.

💻 2022-2023

The Beginning of My Struggle

During my first year, I felt a mix of confidence and nerves. In the first few weeks, I began to understand the material, and whenever I was confused, I knew my dad would be there to help. However, as the year went on, I started to fall behind and struggled with some topics. Every time I failed a test, I asked myself, "Do I see myself doing this as a full-time job?" Despite wanting to quit, I reminded myself that it was only my first year and that with practice, I could improve. Over the summer, I focused on programming to strengthen the areas I found most challenging.

📉 Fall 2023

The Semester That Changed It All

Although I had struggled in Computer Science from the beginning, Fall 2023 became a turning point. The fast pace and difficult material made it hard to keep up, and while I did well on assignments with support, my low test grades revealed gaps in my understanding. Some teachers even warned me about serious consequences if this continued—which made me question how well I truly understood the material. Among my four classes that semester, Programming III became the hardest course, where I received a final grade of 58, along with low 60s in my other classes. When I saw that grade, I questioned my path.

Did I quit?

🔄 Winter 2024

Choosing a New Path

After a difficult semester, I entered the next one uncertain about my future in the program. Because of the failed class, I could only take one web programming course along with three general education classes. At the same time, I knew that I had to switch programs, so I enrolled in a career exploration workshop at my CEGEP to discover my strengths and weaknesses. The results pointed toward language, communication, and teamwork—areas I hadn't seriously considered before. As I reflected on this, I realized my interests aligned more with the Arts, Literature, and Communication (ALC) program. The decision to switch was difficult, but it felt like a step toward a better fit for me.

🌱 2024-2025

A New Beginning

After switching to the Arts, Literature, and Communication (ALC) program, I felt more comfortable than in Computer Science. The courses matched my communication and teamwork skills. As time went on, I started developing a leadership role in my classes, helping classmates when needed — whether it was sending notes, explaining concepts, or simply being there for them. This motivated me to become an ALC Rep in my third semester of the program (seventh semester overall).

As an ALC Rep, I gave tours to high school students, helped organize a college-wide poster contest with a teacher and students from other programs, and collaborated with other Reps to plan activities for the ALC community.

This year marked a turning point: I was no longer defined by past struggles but by my growth, resilience, and ability to guide and inspire others.

🎓 Winter 2026

A Journey Completed

Now that I am almost finished with CEGEP, I feel more confident in my abilities and more certain of my path. Over my four years in CEGEP, I have grown from someone who felt lost and unsure about my future into a leader and mentor to others. This period marks not only an academic milestone but also personal growth. I feel ready to take on new challenges with the skills I have gained throughout my journey.

Success is not final, failure is not fatal: it is the courage to continue that counts. —Winston Churchill

My story isn’t about staying on the right path—it’s about finding a better one.
