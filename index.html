My CEGEP Journey Timeline

This timeline shows my journey through CEGEP, from when I first got interested in technology to finding a path that fits me better. It includes the challenges I faced along the way and how I grew from them. Overall, it reflects how I came to better understand my strengths and where I see myself going.

✨ Tip: Hover over each event to explore

💡 Pre-CEGEP

The Dream

Growing up, I always had a passion for technology and how computers worked. My dad works in the tech industry, and I would often watch him at work. After I expressed interest in learning how to code, he taught me how to design a website using HTML, the building block of web design. A few years later, during the COVID-19 pandemic, with plenty of free time, my dad suggested I relearn HTML and also teach myself CSS and JavaScript — languages that give a website style and make it interactive. Two years later, in 2022, this passion led me to apply to the Computer Science program at John Abbott College. Before I started, my dad suggested I also learn C#, the main programming language used in the program, to give me a head start.

💻 2022-2023

The Beginnings of Computer Science

During my first year, I felt both confident and nervous. In the first few weeks, I began to understand the material, and whenever I was confused, I knew my dad would be there to help. However, as the year progressed, I began to fall behind and had difficulty understanding the material. Whenever I failed a test, I always asked myself, Do I see myself doing this as a full-time job? Despite wanting to quit, I reminded myself that it was only my first year and that with more practice, I would improve. Over the summer, I practiced programming to strengthen the areas I found most challenging.

📉 Fall 2023

The Semester That Changed It All

Although I had struggled in my Computer Science classes from the beginning, the Fall 2023 semester marked a turning point. The fast pace and difficult material made it hard to keep up with my classmates, many of whom seemed to find the classes easy. With my dad helping me a lot, I did well on assignments, but my low-test grades revealed a gap in my understanding. The difference raised concerns from some teachers, and I was warned that if it continued, there could be consequences. This forced me to question whether I truly understood the material or was simply relying too heavily on support. Among all my courses, Programming III was the one I struggled with the most. I received a final grade of 58, and in my other three classes, I earned final grades in the low 60s. Still, it was the 58 that weighed on me the most, and when I saw it, I realized that something was not working. Did I quit? YES—but not yet!

🔄 Winter 2024

Choosing a New Path

After the challenges of the previous semester, I entered the following semester with uncertainty about my future in the program. Because of the failed class, I was only able to take a web programming class, as well as three general education classes. At the same time, I knew that I had to switch programs, so I enrolled in a career exploration workshop at my CEGEP, to discover my strengths and weaknesses. The results surprised me a little. They pointed toward strengths in language, communication, and teamwork—areas I had not seriously considered before. As I reflected on this, I realized that my interests and abilities aligned more closely with the Arts, Literature, and Communication (ALC) program. The thought of making that decision to switch was difficult, but it felt like a step toward a path that suited me better.

🌱 2024-2025

A New Beginning

After switching to the Arts, Literature, and Communication (ALC) program, I felt more comfortable than in Computer Science. The courses aligned with both my communication and teamwork skills. As time went on, I started developing a leadership role in my classes, helping classmates when needed — whether it was sending notes, explaining concepts, or simply being there for them. This motivated me to become an ALC Rep in my third semester of the program (seventh semester overall). As an ALC Rep, I gave tours to high school students, helped organize a college-wide poster contest with a teacher and students from other programs, and collaborated with other Reps to plan activities for the ALC community. This year marked a turning point: I was no longer defined by past struggles but by my growth, resilience, and ability to guide and inspire others.

🎓 Winter 2026

A Journey Completed

Now that I am almost finished with CEGEP, I feel more confident in my abilities and more certain of my path. During my four years of CEGEP, I have grown from someone who felt lost and was not sure about my future into a leader and mentor to others. This period marks not only an academic milestone but also personal growth, and I feel ready to take on new challenges with the skills I have gained throughout my four years of CEGEP.
